Preventive Cardiology

Prevention is the cornerstone of lasting heart health. Our preventive cardiology program is designed to identify cardiovascular risk factors early, develop personalized risk reduction strategies, and empower you with the knowledge and tools to protect your heart for years to come.

Led by Dr. James Morton, our preventive cardiology team works closely with your primary care physician to create an integrated care plan tailored to your unique cardiovascular risk profile.

  • Comprehensive cardiovascular risk assessment
  • Lipid management and advanced lipoprotein testing
  • Blood pressure optimization
  • Lifestyle counseling — nutrition, exercise, and smoking cessation
  • Diabetes and metabolic syndrome management
  • Coronary calcium scoring coordination

Diagnostic Testing

Accurate diagnosis is the foundation of effective cardiac treatment. Our facility is equipped with state-of-the-art diagnostic technology, allowing us to perform a comprehensive range of cardiac testing on-site. Dr. Anika Patel leads our imaging program with advanced certification in echocardiography and cardiac CT/MRI.

Most diagnostic workups can be completed within our clinic, saving you time and ensuring that your cardiologist has direct oversight of your results from start to finish.

  • Transthoracic and transesophageal echocardiography (TTE/TEE)
  • Exercise and pharmacologic stress testing
  • Nuclear myocardial perfusion imaging (stress test)
  • Holter monitoring and event recording
  • Cardiac CT angiography and calcium scoring
  • Cardiac MRI coordination and interpretation

Interventional Cardiology

When catheter-based treatment is needed, our interventional cardiology team provides expert procedural care using the latest techniques and technology. Dr. Robert Chen leads our interventional program with fellowship training from Vanderbilt University Medical Center and extensive experience in complex coronary interventions.

Our on-site cardiac catheterization lab is equipped with advanced fluoroscopic imaging, allowing us to perform both diagnostic and therapeutic procedures in a comfortable, efficient setting.

  • Diagnostic cardiac catheterization and coronary angiography
  • Percutaneous coronary intervention (angioplasty)
  • Coronary stent placement — drug-eluting and bare-metal
  • Intravascular ultrasound (IVUS) and fractional flow reserve (FFR)
  • Right heart catheterization
  • Structural heart disease evaluation

Electrophysiology

Heart rhythm disorders require specialized expertise. Dr. Patricia Williams leads our electrophysiology program with fellowship training from Emory University and board certification in clinical cardiac electrophysiology. She brings a methodical, patient-centered approach to diagnosing and treating the full spectrum of cardiac arrhythmias.

We offer comprehensive arrhythmia evaluation and management, from initial diagnosis through advanced procedural intervention, all coordinated within our practice.

  • Arrhythmia diagnosis and management — atrial fibrillation, SVT, VT
  • Catheter ablation for atrial fibrillation and other arrhythmias
  • Permanent pacemaker implantation and management
  • Implantable cardioverter-defibrillator (ICD) placement
  • Cardiac resynchronization therapy (CRT)
  • Implantable loop recorder placement for unexplained syncope

Heart Failure Program

Living with heart failure requires a dedicated care team and a proactive management strategy. Our heart failure program provides structured, guideline-directed medical therapy along with regular monitoring, patient education, and coordination with advanced heart failure centers when needed.

We work closely with each patient to optimize medication regimens, manage fluid balance, and identify early signs of decompensation before they require hospitalization.

  • Heart failure diagnosis, staging, and classification
  • Guideline-directed medical therapy optimization
  • Volume status monitoring and diuretic management
  • Cardiac rehabilitation program referrals
  • Device therapy evaluation — CRT, ICD, LVAD referral
  • Coordination with advanced heart failure and transplant programs

Vascular Medicine

Cardiovascular disease often extends beyond the heart. Our vascular medicine services address diseases of the peripheral arteries, carotid arteries, and venous system. We provide thorough diagnostic evaluation and collaborate with vascular surgeons when procedural intervention is indicated.

Patients with peripheral artery disease, carotid stenosis, or venous disorders benefit from the same evidence-based, patient-centered approach that defines all of our cardiac services.

  • Peripheral artery disease (PAD) screening and management
  • Ankle-brachial index (ABI) testing
  • Carotid artery disease evaluation and monitoring
  • Carotid ultrasound and duplex imaging
  • Venous insufficiency and deep vein thrombosis assessment
  • Coordination with vascular surgery for interventional procedures
